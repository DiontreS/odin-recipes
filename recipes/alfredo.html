Alfredo



Description


This creamy, garlicky, smooth Alfredo sauce is the absolute best.
You do not want to use a grated Parmesan that you buy on a shelf.
When you cook with that type, your Alfredo goes from a sauce to a gooey mess.
You want to buy a very good and very hard Parmesan cheese in triangle form.
Also, make sure the cream doesn't boil too much or there will not be enough liquid
to make the Alfredo sauce smooth.


Ingredients

  • 10 ounces of fettucine
  • 1/2 cup of butter
  • 5 cloves of chopped garlic
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 cups of freshly grated parm
  • 2 tablespoons of dried parsley
Directions
  1. Step 1

    Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
    Add pasta and cook for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente; drain.

  2. Step 2

    Meanwhile, in a large skillet melt butter and add chopped garlic.
    Cook on low for about 5 minutes, stirring often to prevent garlic from burning.

  3. Step 3

    Pour about a 1/4 cup of heavy cream into a small bowl. Add egg yolk and beat
    together; set aside.

  4. Step 4

    Pour remaining cream into the skillet. Increase heat to medium-high. As cream
    starts to boil, mix rapidly using a whisk. Add egg mixture slowly to prevent curdling, whisking
    until well blended. Stir in 1 cup Parmesan cheese until combined.

  5. Step 5

    Add remaining Parmesan cheese and parsley; mix until smooth. Remove from heat
    and serve over cooked pasta.
