Alfredo



Description


This creamy, garlicky, smooth Alfredo sauce is the absolute best.
You do not want to use a grated Parmesan that you buy on a shelf.
When you cook with that type, your Alfredo goes from a sauce to a gooey mess.
You want to buy a very good and very hard Parmesan cheese in triangle form.
Also, make sure the cream doesn't boil too much or there will not be enough liquid
to make the Alfredo sauce smooth.


Ingredients

  • 10 ounces of fettucine
  • 1/2 cup of butter
  • 5 cloves of chopped garlic
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 cups of freshly grated parm
  • 2 tablespoons of dried parsley
Directions
  1. Step 1

    Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and
    bring to a rolling boil over high heat.

  2. Step 2

    Coarsely chop the tomato and transfer to a small bowl.
    Sprinkle the tomato chunks with basil, olive oil, and salt. Set aside.

  3. Step 3

    Once the water is boiling, stir in the rigatoni, and return to a boil.
    Cook the pasta uncovered, stirring occasionally, until the pasta has cooked through,
    but is still firm to the bite, about 12 minutes. Drain well in a colander, and then return
    the pasta to the pot.

  4. Step 4

    Pour the tomato mixture over the pasta and toss to mix.
